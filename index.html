About

Hi, welcome to my personal webpage! I am an hybrid scientist, a bit physicists and material scientist, but mostly physical chemist. I graduated in Condensed Matter Physics in 2018 at Universita Roma Tre. I am originally from Manziana that is still one of my favourite places in the world!

After my degree I moved to London to do a PhD in Physics (again!) in UCL, that has been awarded both departmental and faculty prizes for best thesis. I then finally moved from a Physics department to work in Oxford Materials in the group of Mauro Pasta for then starting my indipendent research career as a EPSRC postdoctoral fellow first, and as a Glasstone Research Fellow after in the Physical and Theoretical Chemistry Lab at University of Oxford. I am also a Junior Research Fellow in The Queen's College and a Stipendiary Lecturer in Brasenose College.

My research interests revolve around the impact of weak intermolecular interactions in simple liquid systems. Understanding liquids behaviour is already very challenging, I am trying to make it easier for you by providing a database of pure liquids and mixtures that you can use to facilitate your research. I imagined you could compare these data to simulations, improve structural predictions, use it as a starting point for your calculations.

You can find my work on GitHub.
